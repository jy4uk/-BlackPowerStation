Curating Sound: Art, Ethnography, and Practice

The Sounds of Protest

MUSI 3570

"Throughout the struggle there was music". This semester, our group was tasked with defining and thinking critically about what the sounds of protests are. Specifically, we were asked to reflect on the ways music and sound is and has been used in South Africa during the fight against the apartheid, what a protest sounds like, and the images that we conjure when thinking about protest.

The Black Power Station

The Black Power Station is a community space located in Grahamstown/Makhanda, South Africa founded by Xolili ("X") Madinda. X is an artist, social activist, and educator. Throughout the semester, our class has had the privilege to communicate with X over Zoom calls and learn from his expertise. The Black Power Station provides a space for artists to create, collaborate, learn, and grow. (More info will be added here!)
